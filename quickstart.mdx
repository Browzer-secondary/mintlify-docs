---

This guide walks you through everything from signing up to sharing your first published workflow doc. By the end, you'll have a live help portal with real, screenshot-rich documentation — generated automatically from a single browser recording.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up for Browzer">
    Go to [trybrowzer.com](https://trybrowzer.com) and click **Start Free**. Enter your email and create a password to open your account. No credit card is required — every new account includes a 14-day free trial.

    <Tip>
      You can also book a personalized 30-minute demo with the Browzer team at [trybrowzer.com/contact](https://trybrowzer.com/contact) if you'd like a guided walkthrough before diving in.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install the Browzer Chrome Extension">
    Browzer captures browser workflows through its Chrome extension. After signing in, navigate to the **Extension** tab in your dashboard and click **Download Extension** to install it from the Chrome Web Store. Once installed, pin the Browzer icon to your Chrome toolbar for quick access.

    <Note>
      Browzer requires Google Chrome. The extension only activates on standard `http://` and `https://` pages — it won't appear on browser-internal pages like `chrome://settings`.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Record your first workflow">
    Navigate to any web page you want to document — an internal tool, a SaaS app, a customer-facing portal, or anything else you do in the browser. Click the **Browzer icon** in your toolbar, then click **Start Recording**. Perform your process as you normally would: navigate pages, click buttons, fill out forms. When you're finished, click **Stop Recording** and give the recording a name and optional description.

    <Tip>
      Browzer captures clicks, form inputs, navigation events, keyboard shortcuts, tab switches, and file uploads. For best results, perform each step deliberately — avoid rushing through the workflow.
    </Tip>

    <Note>
      Documentation generation typically takes 10–30 seconds after you stop recording. For longer workflows (20+ steps), allow up to a minute.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and edit the auto-generated documentation">
    Open your recording from the **Workflows** page in the dashboard. Browzer has already generated a numbered, step-by-step SOP with screenshots, action-type badges (Click, Type, Navigate, and so on), plain-English descriptions, and AI-generated tips for complex steps.

    Review each step and make any edits you need — update a description, add a custom note, or clarify a tip. Changes save automatically and are visible to everyone with access to the workflow.

    <Tip>
      Switch to **Inspector mode** if you need to see the underlying technical details — CSS selectors, element attributes, and raw event data — for any recorded action.
    </Tip>

    <Warning>
      If a step description looks off, check that the target page didn't change between recording and generation. Re-record just that step if needed, or edit the description manually.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a Docs Portal">
    In the dashboard sidebar, click **Docs Portal**, then click **New Portal**. Fill in the required fields:

    - **Title** — the name of your help portal (e.g., "Product Guides" or "User Handbook")
    - **URL slug** — a lowercase, hyphen-separated identifier (e.g., `product-guides`). This becomes part of your portal's public URL.
    - **Description** — a short summary that tells visitors what the portal covers
    - **Primary color** — your brand's hex color code (e.g., `#FF6B35`) to match the portal to your visual identity

    Click **Save** to create the portal.

    <Note>
      Your URL slug must be unique and contain only lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens — no spaces or special characters.
    </Note>

    <Tip>
      Bookmark the Docs Portal page in your dashboard if you plan to manage multiple portals or publish new docs frequently.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish your first doc">
    From your Docs Portal dashboard, select the workflow page you want to publish. Click **Publish** to make it live. The article will appear immediately in your portal, complete with its screenshots, numbered steps, and any edits you made.

    <Tip>
      You can publish articles individually — no need to publish in batches. Use the single-article publish option to get critical docs live quickly without waiting for a full review cycle.
    </Tip>

    <Warning>
      Published articles are publicly accessible at your portal URL. Before publishing, make sure any sensitive information (passwords, personal data, internal URLs) has been removed or redacted from the documentation.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share with your team or customers">
    On any published article page, click the **Share** button to copy a direct link. Send this link to teammates for internal onboarding, or embed it in customer-facing resources. Recipients can view the full step-by-step documentation without needing a Browzer account.

    <Tip>
      Team members on Business plans can also query your published portal directly from Slack using Browzer's Slack help center bot — great for answering process questions without leaving the conversation.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
